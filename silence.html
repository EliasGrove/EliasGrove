Silence

I

Always your suffering will remind the human soul That no burden will be heavier Than the weight of a past, That which will never be forgotten. Life, fate, quickly turn the most simple smile into fallen tears, But what life would life have without lost hopes? Man awakens to life Only when it is already lost.

II

The light song of a child sounds Through the quiet night, And I already feel the vague hope That one can still be happy. Like the inconsistency of a verse, The eternal mystery of life envelops the soul With the useless weight Of itself. How I envy that song of yours through the cold night, Child who sings to me without knowing, I envy you for being content, Being content just by being.

III

How many scorching deserts will I have to cross, How many times walk through worlds that no one has ever seen, How many torments will I have to go through, To have a brief moment of silence?

IV

Alone I walk to the darkness, Deep into the shadows of myself. How soft, how brief, Life gives her sweet fragrance. Sing, sing along By the dark night, Sing the glory of a fight That I have lost. Sing that sad song. Sing that alone I walk that lonely path Where the moon is my only company. Nothing to hear, to feel, nothing to see, Only the certain promisse of death. So I fool the very time, Life itself! Even if I know That I fool nothing but myself, let me sing my poor verse, Let me be without sorrow.

V

Lisbon of tired body, While you bind to distant Memories that still gaze of yours, You have me walking your streets in the morning. From infinity sounds the brief rumor Of the morning. Hopes and useless waits The light wind carries to me once again Through the beautiful dawn of wheat fields, And I feel eternal peace in everything I look at, For from life I want nothing but what life has to give me, Everything else is a dream, And I am tired of dreaming.

VI

After walking through life in this state And from fatigue, After what has already been lost And today is verse. After the wait And being alone, After the life that had ended, What will I be more than dust? Over the vague murmur of life, The Eternal scream of time that makes it into nothing.
